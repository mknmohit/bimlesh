## 3. CRITICAL SAFETY ALERTS (drug-drug interactions in this regimen)

### 🩸 MAJOR — Bleeding-risk stack (4 layers)
- **Apixaban (Abaxis 2.5)** + **Diclofenac SR (NAC-SR 75)** + **Bromelain (Disperzyme)** + (mild) **Nortriptyline (Gabapin-NT)**.
- Diclofenac + DOAC pharmacovigilance HR ≈ **3.3** for any bleeding; HR ≈ 2.24 for upper-GI bleed.
- **Pantocid-DSR** (PPI) is intentionally on board to mitigate upper-GI risk — DO NOT stop it.
- Watch daily for: gum/nose/wound bleeding, large new bruises, melena (black stools), haematuria, hematemesis, severe headache + neuro change.

### 😴 MAJOR — Sedation / fall / respiratory-depression stack
- **Gabapin (gabapentin)** + **Gabapin-NT (gabapentin + nortriptyline)** + **Ultracet (tramadol)** + **Aptivate (cyproheptadine — also sedating)**.
- FDA 2019 warning: gabapentinoid + opioid → respiratory depression; ↑ overdose mortality 49 %.
- **NEVER take Ultracet at the same time as Gabapin-NT (bedtime)** — leave ≥2 h.
- Falls = #1 post-TKR complication. Mandatory walker, night-light, bedside commode.

### ⚡ SERIOUS — Serotonin syndrome / seizure threshold
- **Tramadol (Ultracet)** + **Nortriptyline (Gabapin-NT)** = additive serotonergic load + nortriptyline inhibits CYP2D6 (raises tramadol exposure) + both lower seizure threshold.
- Highest-risk window: first 48 h of any new Ultracet use.
- Red flags: tremor, sweating, fever, agitation, hyperreflexia, twitching, confusion → STOP Ultracet, call doctor.

### ❤ MODERATE — QT prolongation
- **Domperidone (in Pantocid-DSR)** + **Nortriptyline (in Gabapin-NT)** are both QT-prolonging.
- Female sex + post-op + possible PPI-induced low magnesium → reasonable to obtain a baseline ECG and serum K⁺/Mg²⁺/Ca²⁺ at the 1-week follow-up.

### 🦴 MODERATE — Cumulative vitamin-D + calcium load (hypercalcemia risk)
- **Calgrow Forte HD** (calcitriol 0.25 mcg/day) + **Vitomax** (if Form A: another 0.25 mcg/day calcitriol) + **Dee Time 60K** (60,000 IU cholecalciferol/wk) + **Terifrac** (PTH analog → raises serum Ca).
- Cholecalciferol 60,000 IU/wk × 6 wk is an ESTABLISHED loading regimen — safe in this window.
- BUT: stacking **active calcitriol** on top is the concern. Check serum calcium at 4–6 weeks. Symptoms = polyuria, thirst, nausea, constipation, confusion.

### ⏰ ABSORPTION INTERFERENCE (timing-spacing rules)
| Pair | Mechanism | Required spacing |
|---|---|---|
| Pantocid → Rockcef-CV | PPI raises gastric pH → cefuroxime AUC ↓ ~40% | ≥ 2 h |
| Calgrow Forte HD → Gabapin (gabapentin) | Polyvalent cation chelation | ≥ 2 h (Calgrow given AT LEAST 2 h after gabapentin) |
| Calgrow Forte HD → Rockcef-CV | Calcium chelation | ≥ 2 h |
| Rockcef-CV → Cyraflora (bacterial strains) | Antibiotic kills probiotic | ≥ 2 h |

## 11. CRITICAL RULES (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

1. **APIXABAN (Abaxis 2.5) — STRICTLY every 12 hours: 8:30 AM and 8:30 PM.** Drift > 2 h alters anticoagulation. If a dose is missed and < 6 h late → take immediately. If ≥ 6 h late → SKIP and resume next dose. **NEVER double up.**
2. **PANTOCID-DSR — taken at 6:00 AM on waking, swallowed with the FIRST of 3 glasses of lukewarm water (the patient's daily morning hydration ritual).** Plain water does NOT break the empty-stomach rule (it has no calories, no protein, doesn't activate proton pumps, doesn't compromise the enteric coating). NO food / tea / milk / juice / biscuit until breakfast at 8:00 AM. Taking Pantocid AFTER food cuts efficacy in half.
3. **NAC-SR (Diclofenac SR) — ALWAYS within 5–10 min of finishing breakfast and dinner.** Even one fasting dose can cause acute gastric pain or ulcer in this NSAID + anticoagulant context.
4. **DISPERZYME + APTIVATE — must be 30 minutes BEFORE meals on an empty stomach** (i.e., at 7:30 AM, 1:30 PM, 7:30 PM). Their efficacy depends on this.
5. **TERIFRAC — refrigerate at 2–8 °C at all times** except during the injection itself (out of fridge ≤ 5 minutes). NEVER FREEZE. Discard the pen 28 days after first use even if not empty. Sit/lie for 15 minutes after each injection (orthostatic hypotension).
6. **DEE TIME 60K — strictly once per week, on the SAME DAY each week.** Fixed day: **EVERY MONDAY immediately after lunch** (6 doses total: **20-Apr, 27-Apr, 04-May, 11-May, 18-May, 25-May 2026**). Day 1 of the regimen (20-Apr) is itself a Monday — so dose #1 starts immediately.
7. **NaturoLax — must be taken with a FULL glass of water and NOT within 2 hours of any other oral medicine.** Inadequate water = choking / bowel-impaction risk.
8. **Ultracet at 11 PM with Gabapin-NT = FORBIDDEN.** This combination has caused respiratory depression and serotonin syndrome. Use Ultracet only during the day (last dose ≤ 7:00 PM).
9. **Cyraflora — at least 2 hours away from Rockcef-CV** (otherwise the bacterial strains in the probiotic are killed by the antibiotic). In this schedule: 11 AM Cyraflora is 2 h 30 min after AM Rockcef ✓; 11 PM Cyraflora is 2 h 30 min after PM Rockcef ✓.
10. **Calgrow Forte HD — at least 2 hours away from Gabapin** (calcium chelates gabapentin and reduces absorption). In this schedule: 2:30 PM Calgrow is 6 h after AM Gabapin and 6 h before PM Gabapin ✓.
11. **Pantocid → Rockcef-CV gap ≥ 2 hours** (PPI raises gastric pH and reduces cefuroxime absorption ~40 %). In this schedule: 6:00 AM Pantocid → 8:30 AM Rockcef = 2 h 30 min ✓.
12. **Total daily paracetamol from all sources ≤ 3 g.** Each Ultracet has 325 mg. DO NOT take any OTC fever / cold / flu / pain product without first checking with a pharmacist.
13. **DO NOT abruptly stop** Pantocid, NAC-SR, Gabapin, Gabapin-NT, or Terifrac — taper only on doctor advice.

## 12. CAN BE FLEXIBLE (safe adjustments allowed)

- **Pantocid timing** — anchored to the 6:00 AM morning water ritual (taken with the first glass of lukewarm water). In Phases 2+ once Rockcef-CV stops, Pantocid could theoretically be shifted later (7:00–7:30 AM, 30–60 min before breakfast), but keeping it at 6:00 AM with the daily water habit is strongly preferred for habit consistency and missed-dose prevention.
- **Morning water ritual** — 3 glasses lukewarm water at 6:00–6:10 AM. Acceptable to drink slightly more (4 glasses) if patient wants. Acceptable to take Pantocid with glass 1 OR after glass 3 — both pharmacologically equivalent. Do NOT substitute tea / coffee / juice / milk in this slot.
- **Vitomax, Limcee** — clock time of day is flexible by ± 30 min within the meal slot. Both must still be taken immediately after food.
- **Calgrow Forte HD** — can be moved to dinner instead of lunch, *as long as* it stays ≥ 2 h away from Gabapin. Do NOT take Calgrow at 8:30 PM with the Gabapin dose. If moved to dinner, take Calgrow at ~10:30 PM with a glass of milk (after Gabapin's 8:30 PM dose, before the 11 PM bedtime).
- **Cyraflora** — can be ± 30 min around the suggested 11 AM and 11 PM slots, provided the 2-hour gap from Rockcef-CV is preserved (so Cyraflora must not move EARLIER than 10:30 AM or 10:30 PM).
- **Disperzyme + Aptivate** — pre-meal timing of 30 min is the manufacturer's standard, but the **2-h-after-meal slot is also valid** if the pre-meal slot is missed. Skip rather than double if a dose is missed by > 4 h.
- **Dee Time 60K weekly day** — Monday is fixed because Day 1 (20-Apr) is itself a Monday. Any single fixed weekday is pharmacologically acceptable, but do not change the day week-to-week once chosen.
- **Terifrac time** — 9:00 PM is recommended (orthostatic safety + sleep coverage; family attendant present in evening). Any consistent time within ± 2 h of the chosen slot is acceptable, **but it must remain the same time each day**.
- **Bedtime drift** — Gabapin-NT and bedtime Cyraflora can shift up to ± 1 h around 11 PM if patient sleeps earlier or later that night.
- **Ultracet (PRN)** — daytime timing of PRN doses is fully flexible, just respect the 4–6 h inter-dose gap and the 7 PM cut-off.
- **Post-meal cluster timing** — "immediately after food" means within 5–10 min of finishing the meal. If the patient eats slowly, the cluster shifts naturally (e.g., breakfast finishes at 8:35 AM → meds at 8:40 AM is fine).

## 13. COMMON SIDE EFFECT CLUSTERS (grouped, monitor as packages)

### A. 🩸 Bleeding cluster (Days 1–14 most intense)
**Drugs involved:** Apixaban + Diclofenac SR + Bromelain (Disperzyme) + Nortriptyline.
**Watch for:** new large bruises, blood in urine, black or tarry stools, blood in vomit, gum/nose bleeding lasting > 5 min, severe headache or sudden weakness on one side, swelling around surgical wounds beyond expected.
**Action:** Stop bleeding-risk drug suspected (NAC-SR or Disperzyme first; Abaxis only on doctor advice) → call doctor → CBC.

### B. 😴 Sedation / fall cluster (most intense at night and first-dose Ultracet)
**Drugs involved:** Gabapentin + Nortriptyline + Tramadol + Cyproheptadine (Aptivate, Days 1–7) + Domperidone.
**Watch for:** unsteadiness on standing/walking, drooping eyelids, slurred speech, very slow breathing (< 10/min), patient impossible to wake.
**Action:** No driving. Walker mandatory. Bedside commode at night. Night-light. Family attendant within earshot at night. If respiratory rate < 10 → emergency.

### C. 🧠 Serotonin / seizure cluster (highest risk: first 48 h of Ultracet use)
**Drugs involved:** Tramadol + Nortriptyline.
**Watch for:** sudden tremor, sweating, restless agitation, fever, fast heart rate, muscle jerks (myoclonus), exaggerated reflexes, dilated pupils, confusion. Or: an unexplained generalized seizure.
**Action:** STOP Ultracet immediately → cool patient → emergency room.

### D. 💧 Anticholinergic cluster (Days 1–7 most intense due to Aptivate)
**Drugs involved:** Cyproheptadine (Aptivate) + Nortriptyline (Gabapin-NT).
**Watch for:** severe dry mouth, blurred vision, trouble passing urine (no urine for 6+ h), abdominal distension with no bowel motion, confusion.
**Action:** Hydrate. If urinary retention → catheterise / ER. After Day 7 (Aptivate stops) the cluster eases.

### E. 💩 Constipation cluster (highest in Phase 1 & 2)
**Drugs involved:** Tramadol (opioid) + Nortriptyline (anticholinergic) + Cyproheptadine + immobility.
**Watch for:** no bowel motion for 48 h, hard pellet stools, abdominal bloating, painful straining.
**Action:** Start Duphalac → escalate to NaturoLax → escalate to surgeon-prescribed stimulant (e.g., bisacodyl) if persists.

### F. 🦴 Hypercalcemia cluster (cumulative — Phase 1 onwards, peaks at Week 4–6)
**Drugs involved:** Calcitriol (Calgrow ± Vitomax-A) + Cholecalciferol (Dee Time 60K) + Teriparatide.
**Watch for:** unusual thirst, frequent urination especially at night, nausea, vomiting, constipation, lethargy, confusion, muscle weakness, bone pain at NEW sites.
**Action:** Check serum calcium at week 4 → if symptomatic, hold Dee Time 60K and call doctor.

### G. ❤ QT prolongation / cardiac cluster
**Drugs involved:** Domperidone (in Pantocid-DSR) + Nortriptyline (in Gabapin-NT).
**Watch for:** palpitations, light-headedness, fainting, fast/irregular heartbeat.
**Action:** Baseline ECG + electrolytes (K, Mg, Ca) at first follow-up. Avoid OTC azithromycin, ondansetron, prochlorperazine without medical advice.

### H. 🩺 Renal stress cluster
**Drugs involved:** Diclofenac SR + Tramadol + dehydration + reduced mobility.
**Watch for:** decreased urine output (< 500 mL/day), ankle swelling, sudden weight gain, BP rise.
**Action:** Maintain ≥ 1.5–2 L plain water daily. Get serum creatinine at the 1-week visit.
