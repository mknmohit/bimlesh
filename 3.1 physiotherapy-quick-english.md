## 1. Safety rules (memorise these 10)

1. **Never put a pillow under the knees.** Under the calves or heels is fine. Under the knees causes a lifelong limp.
2. **No squatting, kneeling, floor-sitting — forever.** Use a commode chair, not a squat toilet. No vajrasana, no cross-legged, no sashtang pranam.
3. **No jumping, running, rope-skipping — forever.** She can walk, cycle, swim later.
4. **Walker stays for 4 to 6 weeks** (till surgeon clears). Both feet inside the frame always. Stand tall, look forward, not down.
5. **Sit-to-stand: push ARMRESTS, never pull the walker.** The walker tips forward.
6. **Pain ladder: 0 to 3 = safe. 4 = reduce range. 5+ = stop for today.** If pain lasts more than 2 hours after a session, the dose was too big.
7. **Never hold the breath during exercise** (she is on apixaban till 3-May). Hum or count out loud.
8. **Ice 15 to 20 min after every session**, wrapped in cotton cloth. Never on bare skin. Elevate legs between sessions.
9. **Knee straightening matters more than bending in Week 1.** A knee that cannot fully straighten causes a permanent limp.
10. **If in doubt, stop and call.** Ice, elevate, phone Mrs. Kiran.

**For her low back pain:** No prone lying (face-down). No double knee-to-chest. Use a small rolled towel under the low back during all supine work. Log-roll to turn in bed (knees, hips, shoulders move as one unit).

**Best exercise windows:** 10:00 to 11:30 AM (after the 8:30 AM NSAID absorbs) and 1:30 to 4:30 PM (NSAID peak). Avoid exercise within 4 hours of any Ultracet dose and after 9 PM.

## 2. Red flags — STOP and CALL

**Call Mrs. Kiran / Dr. Arora / Max Emergency immediately if:**
- Sudden shortness of breath or chest pain → possible pulmonary embolism
- Calf becomes hot, swollen, or painful in one leg → possible DVT
- Wound bleeding, pus, redness spreading, or fever > 38 °C → possible infection
- Knee gives way or buckles during exercise
- Sudden large swelling (> 2 cm more than yesterday morning)
- Black stool, blood in urine, unstoppable bleeding anywhere (apixaban alert)
- Fall, even if she feels fine afterwards

## 6. Exercise library — how each one is performed

> **Format.** Each exercise shows: position → steps → dose → one key safety rule.

---

### Ex 1 — Deep belly breathing

**Starts:** Day 1 • **Purpose:** Re-open lung bases, calm pain, activate core.

**Position:** Lie on back, thin pillow under head, small rolled towel under low back, legs straight with heels down (no pillow under knees). Right hand flat on belly below navel, left hand on chest.

**How to perform:**
1. Breathe in slowly through the nose for 4 seconds. The belly rises and lifts the right hand. Chest hand barely moves.
2. Pause for 1 second at the top. No straining.
3. Breathe out slowly through softly pursed lips for 6 seconds. Belly falls.
4. Pause 1 second at the bottom.
5. Repeat — 10 breaths is one set.

**Dose:** 10 breaths × 4 sessions/day (waking, pre-lunch, late afternoon, bedtime). Progress to 4-sec in, 8-sec out by Week 2.

**Avoid:** Holding the breath at the top (apixaban bleeding risk). If dizzy, slow down.

---

### Ex 2 — Ankle pumps

**Starts:** Day 1 • **Purpose:** Prevent DVT. Most important Day 1 exercise.

**Position:** Lie on back (or sit in chair with legs on footstool). Both legs straight, toes up, thighs and knees still.

**How to perform:**
1. Pull both feet up toward the nose (2 sec) — gentle stretch in the back of calves.
2. Push both feet down like pressing a brake pedal (2 sec) — calf muscles harden.
3. Repeat — one full pump = ~4 seconds.
4. Do 20 pumps per leg. Can do both feet together or one at a time.

**Dose:** 20 pumps/leg every 1 to 2 hours while awake (8 to 12 sessions/day in Weeks 1 to 2). Continue until all swelling is gone (~3 months).

**Avoid:** Toes-only wiggle; the whole ankle must move. Sudden sharp pain in one calf → STOP, compare both calves, call doctor.

---

### Ex 3 — Ankle circles

**Starts:** Day 2 • **Purpose:** Keep ankle joint mobile so she doesn't wobble on the walker.

**Position:** Same as ankle pumps — on back, legs straight, thighs still.

**How to perform:**
1. With the right foot, draw a big slow circle with the big toe as if on the ceiling (4 seconds per circle).
2. Do 10 clockwise circles.
3. Reverse — 10 anti-clockwise circles.
4. Switch to the left foot.

**Dose:** 10 CW + 10 ACW each foot × 3 sessions/day, paired with ankle pumps.

**Avoid:** Tiny fast circles (ineffective). Cramp → stop, rub foot, sip water.

---

### Ex 4 — Leg elevation

**Starts:** Day 1 • **Purpose:** Drain swelling from the knees.

**Position:** Lie on back. Stack 4 firm pillows (~25 to 30 cm tall) at the foot end to support BOTH calves from mid-calf to heel. **Back of the knees stays in the air with a 4 to 5 cm gap above the pillows.** Thin pillow under head. Rolled bath towel under low back. Toes up, feet visible.

**How to perform:**
1. Build the pillow stack before lying down. Ankles must be 15 to 30 cm above the chest.
2. Lie back, settle for 1 minute, take 3 belly breaths.
3. Every 3 to 5 minutes during the session, do 10 ankle pumps.
4. Stay 20 to 30 minutes. Can listen to radio or do Kegels.
5. To get out: slide one foot off, then the other, then log-roll onto the side, push up with arms.

**Dose:** 20 to 30 min × 5 sessions/day in Week 1; 3 to 4 × Week 2; 2 × Weeks 3 to 6; 1 × Week 7 to 12.

**Avoid:** **NEVER place a pillow directly under the back of the knee.** Causes permanent limp. Do NOT sleep overnight in this position.

---

### Ex 5 — Quadriceps sets (static thigh squeeze)

**Starts:** Day 1 • **Purpose:** Wake up the thigh muscle that "goes to sleep" after surgery. Foundation for safe walking.

**Position:** Lie on back, legs straight, toes up. Small 3 cm folded towel under each HEEL (so the back of the knee can press down into the mattress).

**How to perform:**
1. Right leg first.
2. Tighten the front of the right thigh, push the back of the knee HARD DOWN into the bed. The kneecap slides 1 cm up toward the hip.
3. Hold 5 seconds. Count out loud — do not hold the breath.
4. Release slowly over 2 seconds.
5. Rest 2 seconds. Repeat — 10 reps is one set.
6. Switch to the left leg.

**Dose:**
- Day 1 to 3: 5 reps × 1 set × 3 sessions
- Day 4 to 7: 10 × 2 sets × 3 sessions
- Week 2 onwards: 10 × 3 sets × 3 sessions (hold progresses to 10 sec by Week 3)

**Avoid:** Lifting the leg (that's an SLR). Squeezing only the buttock or heel. Skip on a flare day.

---

### Ex 6 — Gluteal squeezes (buttock clench)

**Starts:** Day 1 • **Purpose:** Wake up the buttock muscles. Helps walking and the low back.

**Position:** Lie on back, both legs long and flat, arms beside body.

**How to perform:**
1. Squeeze BOTH buttocks together as if pinching a pencil between them.
2. Hold 5 seconds. Count out loud.
3. Release slowly.
4. Repeat 10 times.

**Dose:** 10 reps × 2 sets × 3 sessions/day from Day 4. Progresses to 3 sets.

**Avoid:** Breath-holding. If LBP worsens, ease the squeeze.

---

### Ex 7 — Heel slides (active knee bending)

**Starts:** Day 1 • **Purpose:** Restore knee bending. Target by Day 14: 90° both knees.

**Position:** Lie on back, both legs long and flat, low-back towel in place, toes up.

**How to perform:**
1. Right leg first. Slide the right heel slowly along the bed toward the buttock, bending the knee.
2. Stop at the first strong stretch (NOT pain).
3. Hold 3 seconds at the bent end.
4. Slide the heel slowly back to fully straight. Hold straight 2 seconds.
5. Repeat 5 to 10 times, then switch legs.

**Dose:**
- Day 1: 5 reps × 1 set × 1 session
- Day 4 to 7: 10 × 3 sets × 3 sessions
- Week 2 onwards: continue + add Ex 18 (towel-assisted) for slowest knee

**Avoid:** Forcing past pain. Heel lifting off the bed (from Day 8 put a smooth bedsheet under the heel). Other leg moving.

---

### Ex 8 — Passive knee extension (heel prop)

**Starts:** Day 2 • **Purpose:** Most important for preventing the knee from staying bent (flexion contracture).

**Position:** Lie on back, low-back towel in place. Place a 3 cm folded towel UNDER THE HEEL ONLY of the working leg. The whole calf is in the air, only the heel rests on the towel. Other leg flat.

**How to perform:**
1. Settle into position. Relax the working leg completely — thigh muscle OFF.
2. Stay relaxed for 5 to 10 minutes. Do belly breathing or Kegels while waiting.
3. Gravity slowly drops the back of the knee toward the bed. **Do NOT push** — just relax.
4. End: gently slide the towel out, sit up via log-roll.

**Dose:**
- Day 2: 3 min/leg × 2 sessions
- Day 5 to 7: 10 min/leg × 2 sessions
- Day 8 to 14: 10 min/leg × 3 sessions
- Week 3+: 15 min/leg × 1 to 2 sessions, optional 1 to 2 kg sandbag on the thigh (NEVER on the kneecap or shin)

**Avoid:** Tensing the thigh. Pillow under the calf (only the heel goes on the towel). No caregiver forcing the knee down while on apixaban.

---

### Ex 9 — Seated knee extension (long-arc quad)

**Starts:** Day 4 (assisted); Day 10 true LAQ • **Purpose:** Active strengthening through full range, builds strength for walking and stairs.

**Position:** Sit upright on a firm armchair (seat ≥ 46 cm high), back against the back-rest, lumbar roll behind L4–L5. Both feet flat on floor, knees at 90°. Hands on armrests.

**How to perform (assisted, Day 4 to 9):**
1. Place the LEFT foot UNDER the right shin.
2. Use the left foot to gently help LIFT the right leg — straighten the right knee slowly.
3. Hold 3 seconds at the top (as straight as she can go, target 0 to 75°).
4. Lower slowly over 3 seconds.
5. Repeat, then switch legs.

**How to perform (true LAQ, Day 10+):**
1. Without help from the other foot, slowly straighten the right knee by tightening the front of the thigh.
2. Hold 3 seconds at the top (knee fully straight).
3. Lower slowly over 3 seconds.

**Dose:**
- Day 4: 5 reps × 1 set/leg (assisted)
- Day 8 to 9: 8 × 2 sets/leg (assisted)
- Day 10+: 8 × 2 sets/leg (true LAQ)
- Week 3: 8 × 3 sets, full range 0 to 90°
- Week 4+: add 0.5 kg (500 mL water bottle taped to ankle)

**Avoid:** Leaning back to swing the leg up. Fast jerky lift. Dropping the leg.

### Ex 10 — Seated knee flexion (heel slide under chair)

**Starts:** Day 4 • **Purpose:** Active knee bending with gravity's help. Reaches 90°+ by Week 2, 110°+ by Week 4.

**Position:** Sit upright in firm chair, lumbar roll behind L4–L5, both feet flat, knees at 90°. Hands on armrests.

**How to perform:**
1. Right foot first. Slide the right foot SLOWLY backward under the chair, bending the knee. Heel drags or lifts slightly.
2. Stop at the first strong stretch (NOT pain).
3. Hold 3 seconds.
4. Slide the foot forward to start.
5. Repeat, then switch.

**Dose:**
- Day 4: 5 × 1 set/leg
- Day 5 to 7: 8 × 2 sets/leg
- Day 8 to 14: 10 × 2 sets/leg
- Week 3+: 10 × 3 sets/leg, 3 to 5 sec hold at bent end

**Avoid:** Leaning forward to fake more bend. The spine stays upright; only the knee bends.

### Ex 11 — Incentive spirometry

**Starts:** Day 1 • **Purpose:** Re-open the lung bases. Prevents post-surgery pneumonia.

**Position:** Sit upright in a chair (or in bed at 45° to 90°). NOT lying flat. Lumbar roll behind L4–L5. Hold spirometer upright.

**How to perform:**
1. Exhale fully through the mouth (empty the lungs).
2. Seal the lips around the mouthpiece.
3. Inhale SLOWLY through the device. Watch the piston rise. Aim for the marked target line.
4. **At the top, HOLD the breath for 3 to 5 seconds.** This is the most important step.
5. Remove the mouthpiece. Exhale gently through pursed lips.
6. Rest 30 seconds.
7. Repeat — 10 sustained breaths.

**Dose:** 10 breaths every 1 to 2 hours while awake for first 7 to 10 days. Taper to 4 sessions/day in Week 2. Stop after Day 14 if no cough/wheeze and chest sounds clear.

**Avoid:** Lying flat. Fast inhale (piston crashes). No hold at the top. Back-to-back breaths without rest (causes dizziness).

### Ex 12 — Walker walking

**Starts:** Day 1 • **Purpose:** Safe walking during the first 4 to 6 weeks.

**Position:** Stand inside the walker frame — **both feet inside**. Stand tall, shoulders back, head up, looking FORWARD (not at the feet). Hands on the grips with elbows slightly bent. Weight balanced through both feet.

**How to perform:**
1. Push the walker forward ~25 to 30 cm (one foot's length).
2. Step forward with the less-painful leg first.
3. Step the other leg forward to meet it — both feet inside the frame.
4. Pause for 1 second.
5. Walker forward again. Repeat.

**Dose:**
- Day 1: 2 to 3 bathroom trips (~100 to 150 steps)
- Day 4 to 7: 4 to 5 trips, one 3-min continuous walk by Day 7
- Week 2: 6 to 8 indoor trips, 5 to 10 min continuous walk
- Week 3: 4 walks × 5 to 8 min indoors
- Week 4: 4 walks × 10 to 15 min; walker → two-sticks trial (if cleared)
- Week 5 to 6: outdoor 10 to 30 min on smooth concrete with caregiver

**Avoid:** Looking down at the feet. Hanging body weight on the walker (push DOWN, don't hang). Stepping outside the frame. Walker too far ahead (> 30 cm). Pulling UP on the walker to stand from a chair — **push the chair's armrests instead**.

**Caregiver:** walk BEHIND her at hip height, hand near her belt, ready to catch.

### Ex 13 — Sit-to-stand (armchair, bilateral TKR version)

**Starts:** Day 3 • **Purpose:** Safe transfer technique. Highest-risk move for bilateral TKR — both knees take weight together.

**Position:** Sit upright in a firm armchair, seat 46 to 50 cm high, armrests, back against the back-rest, lumbar roll in place. Both feet flat, slightly tucked back (heels under knees). Hands on armrests. Walker just outside the chair in front, within easy reach.

**How to perform (sit → stand):**
1. Scoot forward to the front edge of the chair (small bottom-shuffles).
2. Feet flat, slightly tucked back.
3. Lean the chest forward — **"nose over toes"** — spine straight, lumbar roll in place.
4. Hands on the armrests, palms down.
5. Push HARD with both arms on the armrests — both legs straighten together.
6. Stand FULLY upright BEFORE moving the hands to the walker.
7. Then move both hands to the walker for balance.

**How to perform (stand → sit):**
1. Back up until the back of both calves touches the chair.
2. Reach back one hand at a time to the armrests (NOT to the walker).
3. Both hands on armrests.
4. Lean forward slightly, nose over toes.
5. Lower SLOWLY (3 sec) into the seat.
6. Slide back against the back-rest.

**Dose:**
- Day 3: 3 practice reps × 1 set with coaching
- Day 4 to 7: 5 reps/session × naturally during transfers
- Day 8 to 14: 5 to 10/session × 2 to 3 sessions/day
- Week 3+: just integrate into normal life (20+ sit-to-stands naturally)

**Avoid:** **Pulling up on the walker — the walker tips forward, she falls on both knees.** Armrests always. Reaching for the walker mid-stand. Dropping into the chair.

### Ex 14 — Pelvic tilts (posterior)

**Starts:** Day 2 • **Purpose:** Flatten the low-back curve, decompress facet joints, foundation for core work.

**Position:** Lie on back, thin pillow under head, **low-back towel REMOVED** (she needs to feel the back press into the bed). Both knees bent, feet flat on the bed, hip-width apart (hook-lying). Arms beside body.

**How to perform:**
1. Take a slow belly breath in.
2. As you exhale, pull the belt-buckle UP toward the chin and tuck the tailbone DOWN toward the heels. Lower back presses into the bed.
3. Hold the tilt 3 to 5 seconds. Continue breathing normally.
4. Release slowly on the next inhale.
5. Rest 2 seconds. Repeat — 10 reps is one set.

**Dose:**
- Day 2: 5 × 2 sets × 2 sessions
- Day 4 to 7: 10 × 2 sets × 3 sessions
- Week 2 onwards: 10 × 3 sets × 3 to 4 sessions (hold 5 to 8 sec)

**Avoid:** Lifting the hips off the bed (that's a bridge). Breath-holding. Over-bracing the belly.

### Ex 15 — Abdominal 3-in-1 brace (TrA + diaphragm + Kegel)

**Starts:** Day 3 • **Purpose:** Best evidence-based exercise for postpartum chronic LBP. Combines deep-belly draw, breathing, and pelvic-floor lift.

**Position:** Lie on back, hook-lying (knees bent, feet flat). One hand flat on lower belly below navel, other on chest.

**How to perform:**
1. Inhale slowly through the nose (4 sec) — let the belly rise.
2. Begin a slow exhale through pursed lips. During the exhale, do all three together:
   - **Lower belly draws in** 10% (gently, not a hard suck)
   - **Pelvic floor lifts up and in** (like stopping the flow of urine)
   - **Slow exhale continues**
3. Hold the brace 5 to 10 seconds while breathing normally. Talk to the caregiver to prove you are not holding the breath.
4. Release slowly. All three relax together.
5. Rest 5 seconds. Repeat — 5 to 10 reps is one set.

**Dose:**
- Day 3: 5 × 1 set × 5 sec hold × 1 session
- Day 4 to 7: 10 × 1 set × 5 sec × 1 session
- Week 2: 10 × 1 set × 5 to 8 sec × 2 sessions/day
- Week 3+: 10 × 2 sets × 8 to 10 sec × 2 sessions/day, PLUS brace before every sit-to-stand and transfer

**Avoid:** Hard belly suck (10% only). Breath-holding. Squeezing the buttocks instead of the pelvic floor. Upper-belly bulge.

### Ex 16 — Kegels

**Starts:** Day 3 • **Purpose:** Pelvic-floor strength — biggest effect size for postpartum LBP. Also fewer night toilet trips = fewer falls.

**Position:** Any. Easiest lying in hook-lying. Eventually seated, standing, during walks.

**How to perform (slow Kegel):**
1. Inhale slowly.
2. Exhale slowly. As you exhale, squeeze and lift the muscles that stop the flow of urine — internal elevator UP.
3. Hold 5 seconds. Breathe normally.
4. Release slowly over 2 seconds.
5. Rest 3 seconds. Repeat 10 times.

**How to perform (fast Kegel, from Week 2):**
1. Quick squeeze and lift for 1 second.
2. Quick release for 1 second.
3. Repeat 10 times in a row.

**Dose:**
- Day 3: 10 slow × 2 sessions
- Day 4+: 10 slow + 10 fast × 2 sessions
- Week 3+: 15 slow + 15 fast × 2 sessions (hold progresses to 10 sec)

**Avoid:** Squeezing the buttocks or thighs — they should stay relaxed. Breath-holding. Not releasing fully between reps.

### Ex 17 — Hamstring sets (isometric "dig heel down")

**Starts:** Day 3 • **Purpose:** Balance the quad work with back-of-thigh activation.

**Position:** Lie on back, both legs long, 3 cm folded towel under EACH heel. Toes up.

**How to perform:**
1. Right leg first.
2. Press the right heel firmly DOWN into the towel/bed — as if trying to push the bed away with the heel. Back of the thigh tightens.
3. Hold 5 seconds. Count out loud.
4. Release slowly.
5. Repeat, then switch.

**Dose:**
- Day 3: 5 × 1 set/leg × 1 session
- Day 8 to 14: 8 × 2 sets/leg × 1/day
- Week 3+: 10 × 2 sets/leg × 1/day (hold 8 to 10 sec)

**Avoid:** Knee lifting (that's a heel slide). Buttocks dominating. Cramp → stop, bend the knee, sip water.

### Ex 18 — Assisted heel slide (with towel)

**Starts:** Day 8 • **Purpose:** Extra knee bend beyond what active heel slides can reach. Safer than passive stretching during apixaban window.

**Position:** Lie on back, both legs long. Thin pillow under head, low-back towel in place. Long bath towel (~150 cm) looped around the BALL of the working foot, both ends held in HER hands.

**How to perform:**
1. Loop the towel around the ball of the right foot.
2. Slide the right heel back along the bed toward the buttock, bending the knee.
3. Use the towel to GENTLY deepen the bend by 5 to 10° beyond what she could do alone.
4. Stop at the first strong stretch (NOT pain).
5. Hold 10 seconds at the bent end.
6. Slowly slide the heel back to straight, still holding the towel for control.
7. Repeat, then switch legs.

**Dose:**
- Day 8 to 14: 5 × 1 set/leg × 1/day (on slowest-bending knee)
- Week 3+: 8 × 2 sets/leg × 1/day (both knees); hold extends to 20 sec by Week 4

**Avoid:** **Caregiver must NOT pull** during Weeks 1 to 2 (apixaban). Patient controls force. Forcing past pain.

### Ex 19 — Single knee-to-chest stretch

**Starts:** Day 8 • **Purpose:** Gold-standard low-back stretch. Also gentle gravity-assisted knee bend.

**Position:** Lie on back, hook-lying, thin pillow under head, **low-back towel REMOVED** for this one.

**How to perform:**
1. Right knee first.
2. Lift the right foot off the bed and bring the knee toward the chest.
3. Place both hands BEHIND THE THIGH (above the kneecap). **NEVER behind the kneecap itself.**
4. Gently pull the knee toward the chest as far as comfortable. Other foot stays flat on the bed.
5. Hold 20 to 30 seconds. Breathe normally.
6. Release slowly.
7. Switch legs.

**Dose:**
- Day 8 to 14: 3 × 20 sec hold/leg × 1/day
- Week 3+: 3 × 30 sec/leg × 1/day (progressing to 45 sec by Week 4)

**Avoid:** **Hands behind the kneecap** (knee compression). **Both knees together (double knee-to-chest is forbidden in this plan).** Bouncing.

### Ex 20 — Seated hamstring stretch (with belt or towel)

**Starts:** Day 6 • **Purpose:** Lengthen hamstrings (tight hamstrings worsen LBP and limit knee straightening).

**Position:** Sit on the edge of a firm chair OR on the bed with both legs in front. Lumbar roll behind L4–L5. One leg straight, heel on a footstool. Other leg bent. Towel looped around the BALL of the straight foot, both ends in her hands. Sit tall.

**How to perform:**
1. Sit tall, spine straight, lumbar roll in place.
2. Right leg straight, heel on stool.
3. Loop towel around the ball of the foot.
4. Pull the towel gently toward her face — flexes the foot, toes come toward the nose. The LEG STAYS STRAIGHT.
5. Stretch felt at the BACK of the thigh.
6. Hold 30 seconds. Breathe normally.
7. Release slowly. Switch legs.

**Dose:**
- Day 6: 30 sec × 3 reps/leg × 1/day
- Day 8: 30 sec × 5 reps/leg
- Week 3+: 45 sec × 3 reps/leg (progressing to 60 sec by Week 4)

**Avoid:** **Leaning forward to reach the toes** — flares LBP. Use the towel to pull the foot, don't lean. Bending the knee to fake the stretch.

### Ex 21 — Seated cat-cow

**Starts:** Day 12 • **Purpose:** Spinal mobility without kneeling (kneeling is permanently forbidden for her).

**Position:** Sit on the FRONT edge of a firm chair so the back doesn't touch the back-rest. Both feet flat, knees at 90°. Hands on thighs. **Lumbar roll removed for this one.** Spine in neutral. Look forward.

**How to perform:**
1. Start in neutral. Take 1 belly breath.
2. **Cat (round forward):** as you exhale, round the spine — pull the navel back, drop the head, tuck the tailbone. Pause 1 sec.
3. **Cow (arch back):** as you inhale, reverse — lift the chest, look up slightly, tilt the tailbone back, gently arch the spine. Pause 1 sec.
4. Continue the slow flow: cat → cow → cat → cow.
5. Do 5 to 8 cycles. One set is about 1 minute.

**Dose:** Day 12: 5 cycles × 2 sessions/day. Week 3+: 8 cycles × 2 sessions.

**Avoid:** Jerky movement. Hips lifting off the chair — only the spine moves. Breath-holding.

### Ex 22 — Supine hip abduction (isometric)

**Starts:** Day 9 • **Purpose:** Side-of-hip (gluteus medius) activation. Stabilises SI joint, supports gait.

**Position:** Lie on back, both legs long. Place a firm pillow on the OUTSIDE of each thigh — OR husband's hands on the outside of each thigh as resistance.

**How to perform:**
1. Squeeze the OUTSIDE of both thighs outward against the pillows (or hands). Legs don't actually move — this is isometric.
2. Hold 5 seconds. Count out loud.
3. Release slowly.
4. Repeat 8 times.

**Dose:**
- Day 9: 8 × 1 set × 5 sec × 1/day
- Day 11+: 8 × 2 sets × 5 sec × 1/day
- Week 3+: 10 × 2 sets × 8 sec × 1/day

**Avoid:** One side dominating. Sharp hip pain (possible bursitis → stop).

### Ex 23 — Standing weight shift in walker

**Starts:** Day 6 • **Purpose:** First standing balance exercise. Teaches each knee to accept weight safely.

**Position:** Stand inside the walker frame — both feet inside, hip-width apart. Stand tall. Hands on walker grips. Husband in front, ready to catch.

**How to perform:**
1. Slowly shift weight to the RIGHT leg (lift left foot off floor by 1 cm if comfortable, but keep it ready to land).
2. Hold 2 seconds.
3. Shift weight back to both feet equally.
4. Shift to the LEFT leg. Hold 2 seconds.
5. Return to both feet. Repeat — 10 shifts each way = 20 total.

**Dose:**
- Day 6: 10 each way × 1 set × 1/day
- Day 8+: 10 each way × 2 sets × 1/day
- Week 3+: 15 each way × 2 sets × 1/day (5-sec hold, lift 5 cm)

**Avoid:** Quick rocking. Hunching over the walker. Feet outside the frame. **This is NOT formal single-leg stance — that stays gated to Week 5.**

### Ex 24 — Supported standing hip extension

**Starts:** Day 6 • **Purpose:** Glute max activation in standing. Powers forward walking, protects the low back.

**Position:** Stand facing a kitchen counter or table edge at waist height, both hands resting on the counter. Walker beside her for backup. Stand tall, head up. Feet hip-width, knees soft (not locked).

**How to perform:**
1. Squeeze the right buttock and slowly lift the right leg STRAIGHT BACK behind her (5 to 15 cm).
2. Keep the leg straight — **don't bend the knee**.
3. Keep the back upright — **don't arch the back to lift higher**.
4. Hold 2 seconds.
5. Lower slowly.
6. Repeat, then switch legs.

**Dose:**
- Day 6: 5 × 1 set/leg × 1/day
- Day 9+: 6 × 2 sets/leg × 1/day
- Week 3+: 10 × 2 sets/leg × 1/day (hold 5 sec, range 10 to 15 cm)
- Week 4: add soft theraband around the ankle

**Avoid:** **Arching the back to fake a higher lift** — flares LBP. Leaning forward. Knee bending.

### Ex 25 — Mini-bridge (small hip lift, 3 to 5 cm)

**Starts:** Day 7 • **Purpose:** Safe glute bridge for someone with chronic LBP. Strengthens glutes and hamstrings.

**Position:** Lie on back, hook-lying (knees bent, feet flat, hip-width). **Low-back towel removed.** Arms beside body.

**How to perform:**
1. Settle into hook-lying. Take 1 belly breath.
2. Squeeze BOTH buttocks tightly FIRST.
3. As you exhale, lift the hips just 3 to 5 cm off the bed by pressing the feet down. **Lower back stays flat — do not arch.**
4. Hold 3 seconds at the top. Breathe normally.
5. Lower slowly over 3 seconds.
6. Rest 2 seconds. Repeat — 5 to 8 reps is one set.

**Dose:**
- Day 7: 5 × 1 set × 3 sec × 1/day
- Day 9: 6 × 2 sets × 5 sec × 1/day
- Week 3+: 8 × 2 sets × 5 to 8 sec × 1/day

**Avoid:** **Big arch lift** — implants and LBP cannot tolerate it. Range stays at 3 to 5 cm forever. **If LBP > 3/10 with this exercise, skip for 2 days, retry at half range; if still flares, omit permanently** and do more Ex 6 + Ex 24 instead.

### Ex 26 — Short-arc quadriceps (SAQ with towel roll)

**Starts:** Day 5 • **Purpose:** Bridges static quad sets and full SLR. Isolates the quad in a small concentric contraction.

**Position:** Lie on back. Low-back towel in place. Firm rolled bath towel or blanket (~15 to 20 cm diameter) under the right knee so the knee is bent ~30° over the roll. Other leg flat.

**How to perform:**
1. Knee bent over the towel roll.
2. Tighten the front of the thigh.
3. Slowly lift just the lower leg (foot and shin) by straightening the knee. **Thigh stays on the towel.**
4. Aim to fully straighten the knee at the top.
5. Hold 3 seconds at the top (knee fully straight).
6. Lower slowly over 3 seconds.
7. Repeat, then switch.

**Dose:**
- Day 5: 5 × 2 sets/leg × 1/day
- Day 10+: 8 × 2 sets/leg
- Week 3+: 10 × 3 sets/leg (hold 5 sec)
- Week 4: add 0.5 kg ankle weight (if SLR established)

**Avoid:** Thigh lifting off the towel (that becomes SLR). Knee stopping short of fully straight (extension lag — stay with this exercise, don't progress yet).

### Ex 27 — Mini-squats with walker (15 to 30°)

**Starts:** Day 10 • **Purpose:** Functional quad strengthening in standing. Closer to walking and stairs.

**Position:** Stand inside the walker frame, both feet inside, hip-width. Stand tall. Hands on walker grips. Husband nearby.

**How to perform:**
1. Slowly bend both knees 15 to 30° (small dip) — send the buttocks slightly back AND the knees slightly forward. Keep the back straight.
2. Stop at the comfortable bend (NOT a deep squat).
3. Hold 2 seconds.
4. Slowly straighten back to standing.
5. Pause 2 seconds. Repeat — 5 to 8 reps is one set.

**Dose:**
- Day 10: 5 × 1 set × 1/day
- Day 13+: 8 × 3 sets × 1/day
- Week 3: depth 40°, 8 × 3 sets
- Week 4: 10 × 3 sets

**Avoid:** Going deep. Knees collapsing inward — they track over the toes. Leaning forward. Knee buckle → STOP, don't retry without physio.

### Ex 28 — Calf raises at walker

**Starts:** Day 11 • **Purpose:** Restore calf push-off strength for smooth walking.

**Position:** Stand inside the walker frame (or facing a kitchen counter), both feet flat, hip-width. Stand tall. Hands on walker/counter.

**How to perform:**
1. Slowly lift BOTH heels off the floor together, rising onto the balls of the feet (tiptoe).
2. Hold 2 seconds at the top.
3. Slowly lower both heels back to the floor.
4. Pause 2 seconds. Repeat — 5 to 10 reps is one set.

**Dose:**
- Day 11: 5 × 1 set × 1/day
- Day 12+: 8 × 2 sets × 1/day
- Week 3+: 10 × 3 sets × 1/day
- Week 6: single-leg calf raise with hand support, 8 × 2 sets/side

**Avoid:** One heel rising higher. Quick bouncing. Hunching.

### Ex 29 — Step rehearsal (4 cm block)

**Starts:** Day 12 • **Purpose:** Pattern for stairs. Assesses single-leg loading safely.

**Position:** Stand inside the walker frame. Place a thick book (~4 cm tall, non-slip) about 30 cm in front. Stand tall, hands on walker grips.

**How to perform (Day 12 to 17 — rehearsal only, NO push-up):**
1. Lift the right foot and place it FULLY ON the book (whole foot on).
2. Pause 2 seconds. **Do NOT push body weight onto the book — the walker takes the weight.**
3. Step the right foot back to the floor inside the walker.
4. Repeat 5 times right, then switch to left.

**How to perform (Day 17+ — real step-up):**
1. Place the right foot fully on the book.
2. Push body weight UP onto the right leg, lifting the left foot off the floor. Stand briefly on the right (book) leg.
3. Step the left foot down to the floor.
4. Step the right foot back down inside the walker.

**Dose:**
- Day 12: 5 × 1 set/leg — rehearsal only
- Day 13 to 14: 6 × 2 sets/leg — rehearsal only
- Day 17+: 5 × 2 sets/leg — real step-up
- Week 3: 10 cm step, 5 × 2 sets/leg
- Week 4: 15 cm step
- Week 6: 15 cm step, 8 × 3 sets

**Avoid:** Toes-only on the book. Walker tipping forward. Pushing up before Day 17. Knee buckle → STOP.

### Ex 30 — Single-leg stance inside walker (BILATERAL SAFETY GATE)

**Starts:** Day 29 (Week 5) • **Purpose:** Critical balance milestone. The highest fall-risk move in the plan — delayed to Week 5 on purpose.

**Prerequisites — do NOT start if ANY of these apply:**
- Any extension lag (knee doesn't fully straighten)
- Any SLR lag
- Swelling is more than yesterday morning
- Walker not yet stable for 30-sec double-leg stance

**Position:** Stand inside the walker frame, both feet inside, hip-width. Hands on walker grips. **Husband standing BEHIND her, hands ready to catch at her waist.**

**How to perform:**
1. Slowly shift weight to the LEFT leg.
2. Slowly lift the right foot just 1 to 2 cm off the floor.
3. Hold 5 seconds. Breathe — talk to husband to prove you are not holding the breath.
4. Lower the right foot.
5. Pause 5 seconds with both feet down.
6. Switch: shift right, lift left.
7. Repeat — 3 reps each leg.

**Dose:**
- Day 29: 5 sec × 3 reps/leg × 1/day
- Day 30 to 35: build to 10 sec × 3 reps/leg × 1/day
- Week 6 (by Day 42): 30 sec × 3 reps/leg, eyes open, no hand support

**Avoid:** **Doing it alone — husband must be present always.** Lifting the foot too high. Quick lift and drop. Breath-holding. Proceeding with eyes closed before Week 12.

### Ex 31 — Tandem stance (heel-to-toe)

**Starts:** Day 13 • **Purpose:** Graded balance between two-leg and single-leg stance.

**Position:** Stand sideways to a sturdy kitchen counter or wall, both feet on the floor, both hands on the counter. Stand tall, head up.

**How to perform:**
1. Place the right foot DIRECTLY IN FRONT of the left foot — right heel touching left toes. Both on the same imaginary line.
2. Stand tall, weight equal on both.
3. Hold 10 seconds. Breathe.
4. Step the right foot back to side-by-side.
5. Switch: left foot in front. Hold 10 sec.
6. Repeat 3 times each side.

**Dose:**
- Day 13: 10 sec × 3 each side × 1/day
- Day 14+: 10 to 15 sec × 3 each side
- Week 3: 15 sec × 3, one hand on counter
- Week 4: 20 sec × 3, finger touch only
- Week 5+: no hand support
- Week 6+: tandem walking along a line, 3 m × 3 reps

**Avoid:** Feet apart (not on the line). No hand support too soon. Quick stand and step out — hold for the full count.

### Ex 32 — Chest physiotherapy (percussion + huffing)

**Starts:** Day 1 if cough/wheeze, otherwise 3 times/week preventive • **Purpose:** Clear lung secretions, prevent pneumonia.

**Position:** She sits upright in a chair (or in bed at 60° to 90°). Lumbar roll behind L4–L5 for sessions > 5 min. Hugs a pillow against her chest. Husband sits beside her.

**How to perform:**
1. Husband makes a **CUPPED** hand (not flat) — like cupping a small bowl.
2. Husband gently CLAPS the cupped hand on her upper back (over the shoulder blades, then below them). Rhythmic, ~2 claps/sec. NOT painful.
3. Continue 1 to 2 minutes on each lung area (upper back, mid-back, lower back, sides).
4. After percussion, she takes 3 deep belly breaths.
5. Then she does a HUFF: take a medium breath in, then forcefully huff the air out through an open mouth — like fogging up a mirror. Repeat 3 huffs.
6. If secretions come up, spit into the basin or tissue. Do NOT swallow.
7. Rest 1 minute. Repeat the cycle 2 to 3 times if needed.

**Dose:** 10 min × 1/day if cough/wheeze (continue until cough resolves). 5 min × 3/week preventive for first 2 weeks (stop if no cough, no wheeze, chest sounds clear for 5 days).

**Avoid:** **Flat hand** (causes pain — cupped only). Slapping too hard. Skipping the huff (secretions stay loose but don't move out). Doing it lying down — must be upright.
